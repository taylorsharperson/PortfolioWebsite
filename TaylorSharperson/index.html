Welcome

Greetings, my name is Taylor Sharperson, a first-year CIS major from Morristown, New Jersey. I have three younger siblings, Kennedy, Kenneth, and Tyler, with whom I am very close. My favorite color has been purple forever; even my room has a purple theme. At home, I spend my time journaling and spending time with friends. My friends and I spend much time visiting museums such as the MET in New York.

I chose to major in Computer Information Systems because I want to work in cybersecurity. My mother enrolled me in Black Girls Code, where they taught me simple code, and I have been increasing my skills ever since. I have little experience with HTML, but I am familiar with Java and Python. In high school, I coded multiple games and found that I liked to problem-solve and spend time coding projects. I plan to receive my degree in 4 years and receive employment after graduating.

Outside school, I love shopping, watching Netflix, spending time with friends and family, traveling, and reading. I most often shop online at H&M, Pacsun, and Forever21. My favorite shows on Netflix are the 100, Grey's Anatomy, the Vampire Diaries, and the Originals; I find them very entertaining. This year I have yet to travel as much. I have been to most states and the Bahamas in the past few years. So far, my favorite book this year has been the Inheritance Games, a mystery book.

Black Girls Code

Black Girls Code is a not-for-profit organization that provides technology education for African-American girls. I've enjoyed many years in Black Girls Code. It was one of the first places I learned how to code. I've competed in hackathons and many more during my time. I recommend any young girl interested in code to join. You are not required to attend every event as there are many opportunities ranging from beginner to advanced coding events.

I have recently been chosen to particpate in Black Girls Code as an alumni. This provides me with scholarship opportunities, internship offerings, and opportunities to volunteer, as well as be a mentor for those who are still in the program.
